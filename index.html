How to take a good photo...

Shooting time: 120 minutes
Awesome rating: 100% awesome

Taking a good photo is a great life skill! Wether portraits, a career, or just memorializing your life, taking photos is crucial, and learning how to do it well is worthwhile!

  • Things You'll Need

  • A camera (with SD card and battery)
  • A subject or scene
  • Lighting (optional)

  1. Choose a focus for your photo.

    It is important to have a central focus for your photo so your audience knows what to look at. This item or person should be at the center of the photograph or highlighted in some other way so that they stand out. You can take this literally and use an actual framing object, or you can manipulate your shot in some way instead.[1]

  2. Change up your lighting.

    Identifying where your light is coming from is an important part of ensuring the best lighting for your subject because the light needs to be on your subject to illuminate it properly. Before you take a photo, take a moment to look around you and determine which direction the light is coming from, then adjust your shot if you need to. For example, if you are outdoors, figure out where the sun is and observe how it is casting light on your subject. Consider what angle might be the most flattering or interesting for photographing your subject. If possible, shoot an hour after sunrise or before sunset to get some really dynamic light.

  3. Eradicate red-eye by turning off your flash.

    The red eye is caused by the camera’s flash reflecting off of the subject’s retinas, and it usually happens when you are taking photos in low light.

  4. Make your shot multi-dimensional with foreground and background items.

    If you want your subject to seem small, then you might place something in the foreground of the shot. If you want your subject to look large, then you might place something in the background. If there isn’t any dimension in your photos, they could look a little flat

  5. Get up close and personal to zoom in on your subject.

    Sometimes photos do not look as good as they could because you are too far away from your subject. If you notice that the details of your subject are hard to make out, then try moving a little closer until you like how the photo looks.

  6. Check out your photos as you go and adjust as needed.

    This step can change help fix any situational issues that may pop up.

  7. Enjoy your photos!

    Save your work somewhere secure and enjoy the art and or memories!
